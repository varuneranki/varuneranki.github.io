Why Does Goddess Lakshmi Hold a Veena?

In the Carnatic composition “Varaveena Mrudu Paani” (set in Mohana raga), there is a reference to “Ranganayaki”, which means the consort of Lord Ranganatha (Vishnu), i.e., Goddess Lakshmi. However, this raises an interesting question—why is Goddess Lakshmi depicted holding a veena, an instrument commonly associated with Goddess Saraswati?

Goddess Lakshmi and the Veena

While Saraswati is the primary deity of knowledge and music, Lakshmi in her form as Ranganayaki or Vidya Lakshmi is also depicted holding a veena. This symbolizes her role in bestowing wisdom, prosperity, and harmony together.

In Vaishnavism, Lakshmi is not just the goddess of wealth but also of spiritual knowledge. In this form, she is sometimes shown with a veena, representing divine wisdom along with material prosperity.

Types of Veenas Held by Lakshmi and Saraswati

  • Saraswati's Veena – Known as the Kachchapi Veena or Vipanchi Veena. It is a long, fretted veena with a large resonator.
  • Lakshmi's Veena – Some traditions mention the Lakshmi Veena as a smaller, more decorative veena, symbolizing balance and grace rather than intellectual rigor.

Conclusion

The veena is a symbol of knowledge and harmony, not limited to Saraswati alone. When Lakshmi is shown holding a veena in compositions like Varaveena Mrudu Paani, it signifies her aspect as Vidya Lakshmi, the goddess who grants wisdom along with prosperity.
